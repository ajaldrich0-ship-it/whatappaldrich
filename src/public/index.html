Professional WhatsApp Sender Guide

Industry standards and anti-ban principles to run secure, reliable, and compliant campaigns.

Anti-Ban Campaign Rules

  • Keep Batches Under 50: Split bulk campaigns into segments of 30-50 contacts. Sending 100+ messages instantly triggers automated spam detection.
  • Set High Delay Values: Use a delay of at least 5 to 10 seconds between scheduled messages to simulate normal human typing speed.
  • Personalize Message Text: Include variables like client names or order numbers. Identical broadcast text sent to dozens of numbers is instantly flagged by Meta's AI algorithms.

Trust & Compliance Guidelines

  • Prioritize Consent (Opt-in): Only send messages to clients who have explicitly signed up, ordered, or requested details via your site or business page.
  • Clear Opt-out Signature: Always offer an easy exit in initial outreach (e.g., "Reply 'STOP' to opt out"). If users report/block your chat, Meta will lock your number.
  • Avoid Cold Broadcasts: Sending sales messages to cold lists (unknown numbers) will result in immediate user reports, which is the #1 cause of number blocks.

Automations Best Practices

  • Short trigger keywords: Set simple triggers (e.g., 'address', 'price', 'hours') rather than full sentences, making it easier for customers to auto-trigger responses.
  • Maintain Canned Responses: Keep payment details, physical addresses, and standard refund/return text pre-loaded in "Quick-Replies" to speed up agent workflows.
  • Hybrid Interaction: Keep an eye on incoming logs in the Overview console to step in manually if the bot is unable to resolve a complex inquiry.

Operational Maintenance

  • Persistent Session Caching: The system automatically caches session data, keeping you logged in even after browser closures or backend server restarts.
  • Graceful Logout: Always use the "Disconnect Session" button in Settings if switching phone lines, ensuring WhatsApp registers the logout correctly.
  • Monitor Server Status: Check the status indicator dot in the sidebar (Green = Connected, Yellow = Initializing, Red = Disconnected) for quick diagnostics.
